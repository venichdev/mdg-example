## Writing Guidelines for Conclusion

### Do's ✓
- Keep it concise and focused
- Use clear, direct language
- Emphasize main contributions
- Be honest about limitations
- Connect back to introduction
- End with forward-looking statement
- Be confident but not overstate

### Don'ts ✗
- Don't introduce new information
- Don't include detailed results (just summarize)
- Don't include new citations (except in rare cases)
- Don't apologize for limitations excessively
- Don't be vague about contributions
- Don't repeat Discussion chapter content
- Don't use weak language like "might," "maybe"

## Connection to Introduction

**Your conclusion should mirror your introduction structure:**

| Introduction | Conclusion |
|-------------|-----------|
| General background | Significance and impact |
| Problem statement | Problem addressed (resolved) |
| Research gap | Contribution to filling gap |
| Research questions | Answers to research questions |
| Objectives | Achievement of objectives |
| Thesis organization | Summary of findings |
| (Looking forward) | Future work (continuing forward) |

**Pro tip:** Read your introduction and conclusion together. They should tell
a complete, coherent story of your research journey.
