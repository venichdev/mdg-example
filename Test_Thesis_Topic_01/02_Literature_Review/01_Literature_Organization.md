## Literature Search Strategy

### 1. Search Keywords and Combinations

**Primary Keywords:**
- "torque vectoring" AND "electric vehicle"
- "energy optimization" AND "EV" AND "torque control"
- "autonomous vehicle" AND "torque distribution"
- "ADAS" AND "vehicle control"
- "multi-motor electric vehicle" AND "control"

**Secondary Combinations:**
- "torque vectoring" AND "energy consumption"
- "model predictive control" AND "electric vehicle"
- "optimal torque allocation" AND "EV"
- "wheel torque control" AND "autonomous"
- "differential drive" AND "energy efficiency"

**Advanced Combinations:**
- "torque vectoring" AND "energy" AND "autonomous"
- "real-time optimization" AND "EV" AND "torque"
- "machine learning" AND "torque vectoring" (if applicable)

### 2. Databases to Search

- [ ] **IEEE Xplore** (primary for engineering)
- [ ] **Google Scholar** (broad coverage)
- [ ] **ScienceDirect** (Elsevier journals)
- [ ] **Web of Science** (citation tracking)
- [ ] **Scopus** (comprehensive indexing)
- [ ] **SAE Digital Library** (automotive focus)
- [ ] **arXiv** (preprints in control systems)

### 3. Filters and Criteria

**Time Period:**
- Primary focus: 2019-2024 (last 5 years)
- Seminal papers: Include earlier highly-cited works
- Balance: ~70% recent, ~30% foundational

**Quality Indicators:**
- Peer-reviewed journal articles
- Top-tier conferences (IEEE, SAE, IFAC)
- Citation count (use with caution for recent papers)
- Journal impact factor (supplementary indicator)

## Critical Analysis Framework

For each significant paper, ask:

- [ ] Is the methodology sound and rigorous?
- [ ] Are assumptions clearly stated and reasonable?
- [ ] Is the validation adequate?
- [ ] Are results reproducible?

### Relevance
- [ ] How does this relate to your research?
- [ ] What can you learn or adapt?
- [ ] What gaps does it leave?

### Quality Indicators

- [ ] Comprehensive literature review?
