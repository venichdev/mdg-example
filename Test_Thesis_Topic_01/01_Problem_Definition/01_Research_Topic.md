- Vehicle dynamics and stability
- Energy consumption and range
- Safety and comfort
- Autonomous driving performance

## 2. Problem Background

### Current State of Technology

**Torque Vectoring in EVs:**
- Multi-motor EVs can independently control torque at each wheel
- Enables superior vehicle dynamics compared to conventional drivetrains
- Can improve handling, stability, and safety

**Energy Consumption Challenges:**
- EV range anxiety remains a major concern
- Torque vectoring systems can be energy-intensive
- Trade-off between performance and efficiency

**Autonomous Vehicles & ADAS:**
- Require precise vehicle control
- Must optimize for safety, comfort, and efficiency
- Need real-time adaptive control systems
